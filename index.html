About The Singing People

We don't call ourselves a choir. We don’t have 'rehearsals' and none of us is any good at reading music. But we do love to sing and chat and drink tea. Sometimes we go out dancing.

We’ve been singing and dancing and chatting and drinking tea since 2011, when a few of us thought it might be fun to start a proper choir. We soon realised that it was more fun just to turn up and sing and dance and drink tea whenever we felt like it.

We also love meeting new people, so click the button below to join in and tell us how to take your tea.

How does it work?

Let us know you're coming and then... just turn up! You don't have to commit to turning up every week, or pay a membership subscription, and we won't ask you to fill in any forms. You don't have to know what your 'range' is or have an prior experience. Just be willing to have a go and have a chat. And if you can bake, even better.

Below are our meet-up and singing times. Once a month we sing, and every couple of months we go to the pub. Those who feel like it can join us for our Big Charity Sing Thing in early December.

Our teachers

Dan Daley

Dan is a singer, choir director, arranger and group singing advocate. He has worked in community centres, schools, offices & prisons encouraging people to use their voice for expression. He's our choir-leader-in-chief and head tea-tester.

Lee Sutenhall

Lee is a singer, songwriter, double bass player, composer and choirleader based in Bath and most notorious as the leader of the Spooky Men's Chorale. He graces us with his considerable presense every couple of weeks and we love him for it!
